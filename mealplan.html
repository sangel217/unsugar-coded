Diabetes Meal Planning


Managing blood sugar levels is the key to living well with diabetes and avoiding some of the more severe health problems it can cause. This means that following a healthful diet is essential for people with diabetes. A healthful balanced meal should contain non-starchy vegetables, protein and high-fiber carbohydrates.


Below are some healthy meal examples for those with diabetes.


Breakfast: Omelet: one whole egg, spinach, mushrooms, bell pepper, ½ cup black beans, 1 cup of berries on the side

Lunch: Salad: 2 cups spinach, ¼ cup tomatoes, 1 oz cheddar cheese, one boiled chopped egg, 2 Tbsp yogurt dressing, ¼ cup grapes, 1 tsp pumpkin seeds, ¼ cup roasted chickpeas

Snack: 1 cup low fat plain Greek yogurt mixed with half a banana

Dinner: 2 oz salmon filet, one medium baked potato, 1 tsp butter, 1 ½ cups steamed asparagus

Diabetes Plate Method


Try using the diabetes plate method when creating meals. The diabetes plate method uses the image of a standard, 9-inch dinner divided as follows:

  • 50 percent non-starchy vegetables
  • 25 percent protein
  • 25 percent high-fiber carbohydrates
